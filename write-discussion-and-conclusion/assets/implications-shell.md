# Implications Shell

Use this scaffold for both theoretical implications and practical implications. They are separate outputs—do not merge them.

---

## Theoretical Implications Shell

```
[CONTRIBUTION TYPE — identify the type of contribution this paper makes.
 Use one of:
 - "fills a gap" (the question was unstudied)
 - "resolves a tension" (prior findings conflict; this paper explains why)
 - "challenges an assumption" (prior work assumed X; this paper shows it is conditional or wrong)
 - "extends a boundary" (a well-established finding holds under new conditions)
 - "introduces a construct" (provides a new conceptual tool)]

[FIRST IMPLICATION — one paragraph per major implication.
 Pattern: "First, this study contributes to [literature stream] by [specific contribution].
 Prior work has [what was known / what was assumed]. Our finding that [result] suggests
 that [implication]. This extends / challenges / refines [specific prior work] by [X]."]

[SECOND IMPLICATION — same structure for the next stream or mechanism.]

[METHODOLOGICAL CONTRIBUTION — if applicable, note any design or measurement innovation
 that is reusable by future scholars.]
```

---

## Practical Implications Shell

Good practical implications are specific about: **who** should do **what** differently, **when**, and **why this paper justifies it**.

```
[AUDIENCE — identify the specific practitioner, policymaker, or decision-maker.
 Avoid "managers." Prefer: "senior managers in high-technology firms," "HR directors
 responsible for onboarding," "venture capitalists evaluating early-stage teams."]

[IMPLICATION — state the actionable takeaway.
 Bad: "Managers should pay attention to knowledge diversity."
 Good: "When assembling project teams for novel problem-solving tasks, managers
 should prioritize cross-functional composition over within-function depth,
 because [mechanism from the paper]."]

[JUSTIFICATION — one sentence tying the implication back to the findings.
 "Our finding that [result] suggests that [effect], implying that [action] is
 likely to [outcome] in contexts similar to our empirical setting."]

[BOUNDARY — note the scope of the implication.
 "However, this recommendation is most applicable when [condition].
 In [other condition], the effect may not hold."]
```

---

## Anti-patterns to avoid

| Type | Anti-pattern | Better |
|---|---|---|
| Theoretical | "This study contributes to the literature." | Name the literature and what changes |
| Theoretical | "Future scholars should study X." | That belongs in Limitations & Future Research |
| Practical | "Practitioners should be aware of..." | Name who, and state what they should do |
| Practical | "Policy implications are important." | Describe the specific policy lever and mechanism |
