# Discussion Block Shell

Use this scaffold when drafting the opening or main body of the discussion section. The goal is to move from "what was found" to "what it means" without re-narrating the results table.

---

## Opening of Discussion Shell

```
[COMPRESSED ANSWER — one to three sentences restating the research question and
 the main empirical answer. This is NOT a results summary; it is the thesis statement
 of the discussion.
 Pattern: "This study investigated [question]. We find that [main answer], providing
 evidence that [brief theoretical implication]."]

[TRANSITION — one sentence that signals the shift from what was found to what it means.
 Pattern: "These findings have several implications for [literature stream]."]
```

---

## Discussion Paragraph Shell (per finding or implication)

```
[FINDING ANCHOR — briefly restate the specific finding (one clause, not a full restatement).
 Pattern: "The positive effect of [IV] on [DV] (Hypothesis X)..."]

[INTERPRETATION — explain what this finding means for the theoretical conversation.
 This is NOT "the result was significant." This is: what does the finding reveal,
 confirm, challenge, or extend in the literature?]

[CONTRIBUTION LINK — connect the interpretation back to the paper's opening contribution promise.
 Pattern: "This extends [prior work] by showing that [specific addition]..."
 or "This challenges the assumption in [prior work] that [assumption]."]

[BOUNDARY — acknowledge any condition under which the interpretation may not hold.
 Keep it brief; detailed boundaries go in Limitations.]
```

---

## Handling Nulls and Surprises

Do not bury null results. Give them a paragraph or at minimum a sentence.

```
[NULL STATEMENT — state plainly that the hypothesis was not supported.]

[INTERPRETATION — what does the null mean theoretically?
 Options: (a) the mechanism is weaker than theorized, (b) a moderating condition is needed,
 (c) the context constrains the effect, (d) measurement explains the null.
 Pick one and argue for it. Do not leave a null un-interpreted.]
```

---

## Checklist

- [ ] Opening names the research question AND the main answer
- [ ] Each paragraph moves from finding → meaning → contribution, not finding → finding → finding
- [ ] Nulls are interpreted, not glossed over
- [ ] Every contribution claim is bounded ("in this context", "for this type of firm")
- [ ] No tables or raw coefficients appear in the discussion
