# Limitations and Future Research Shell

Use this scaffold to write a limitations section that is candid but not self-destructive, and a future research section that grows from the paper's actual findings rather than generic filler.

---

## Limitations Shell

Write 2–3 limitations. More than 4 reads as author insecurity. Choose the limits that are most consequential to the paper's central claim.

```
[LIMIT STATEMENT — state the limitation plainly, in one sentence.
 Pattern: "First, [our data / design / sample / measure] does not allow us to [X]."]

[INTERPRETIVE BOUNDARY — explain what interpretive constraint this creates.
 Pattern: "As a result, we cannot rule out [alternative explanation] / we cannot
 establish [stronger claim] / our findings may not generalize to [context]."]

[PARTIAL DEFENSE — if the limitation does not invalidate the main finding, say so.
 Pattern: "However, [reason why the core result is still informative despite this limit].
 Our robustness checks [reference specific check] also partially address this concern."]
```

---

## Future Research Shell

Future research directions should grow from one of three sources:
1. A limitation of the current study
2. An unexpected finding that opens a new question
3. A boundary condition the current study could not test

```
[SOURCE — identify which of the three sources above this direction comes from.]

[DIRECTION — state the specific question future work could address.
 Bad: "Future research should study [construct] in other contexts."
 Good: "Future research could examine whether [mechanism] operates differently
 in [specific contrasting context], where [condition that may alter the mechanism]."]

[WHY IT MATTERS — one sentence on what answering this question would add.
 Pattern: "Doing so would clarify whether [finding from this paper] is [general principle]
 or [context-specific phenomenon]."]
```

---

## Closing Sentence Options

After limitations and future research, a closing sentence can bridge to the conclusion:

```
"Despite these limitations, [this study / these findings] offer[s] a useful starting point
for understanding [phenomenon], and we hope they invite further work on [question]."

"These limitations notwithstanding, the convergent evidence from [main findings and
 robustness checks] gives us confidence that [core claim]."
```

---

## Checklist

- [ ] No more than 3–4 limitations listed
- [ ] Each limitation states the interpretive boundary it creates (not just "we have a limitation")
- [ ] Each limitation includes a partial defense where applicable
- [ ] Future research directions name a specific question, not a general topic
- [ ] Future research directions are tied to this paper's findings or limits, not generic
- [ ] "Future studies should replicate this in other countries" avoided unless specifically justified
