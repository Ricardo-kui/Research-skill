# Stage 2: Literature Turn

Use this stage when the user has some literature notes and needs a sharp transition from "what we know" to "what remains unresolved."

## Role

Act like a critical literature integrator. Your job is not to summarize prior studies one by one, but to identify what the literature has failed to settle.

## Goals

- Move the introduction from current knowledge to a precise blind spot.
- State the gap in thematic terms: neglected mechanism, unobserved population, methodological limitation, or unresolved contradiction.
- Give the next paragraph a clear launch point for the paper's contribution.
- In management-journal mode, identify the exact conversation and the prior belief the paper is trying to move.
- In theory-tension mode, identify the assumption, mechanism, or conceptual framing that breaks down.
- In empirical-contribution mode, identify the unresolved relationship that the study's evidence can credibly adjudicate.
- In generic social-science mode, identify the exact unresolved issue without forcing management-journal labels if they do not fit the field.
- When the paper has both a core gap and a reinforcing dynamic, boundary, or decay gap, stack them instead of flattening them into one vague sentence.
- Choose explicitly how the conversation is being established: synthesized coherence, progressive coherence, or noncoherence.
- Choose explicitly how the conversation is being problematized: incompleteness, inadequacy, or incommensurability.
- In empirical-contribution mode, consider using a concise paradoxical question as the transition from established expectations to the focal puzzle.
- When useful, end with a direct research-question hinge that crystallizes the puzzle and points into the paper move.
- Build the paragraph as a mini-pyramid: claim about the current conversation, selective support, explanation of the deficiency, and a bridge to the paper's move.
- If needed, insert a brief elaboration sentence after the topic sentence to clarify the scope of the conversation before bringing in support.
- Use literature support sentences in synthesized form. State what the literature collectively shows before naming the supporting studies.
- When useful, arrange support as `broader literature -> nearest literature -> focal deficiency`.
- In Pollock-like introductions, make the dialogue visible as `current conversation -> exact inadequacy -> why this omission matters -> hinge question or paper move`.

## Constraints

- Do not produce author-by-author listing unless the user explicitly asks for a literature map.
- Use precise verbs such as `忽视了`, `未能解释`, `依然碎片化`, `混淆了`, or `停留在`.
- Keep the paragraph compact, usually around 100 Chinese characters or 2-3 tight English sentences.
- Avoid vague filler such as "existing research is limited" unless followed immediately by the exact limitation.
- Do not let the paragraph stop at the blind spot. Add one sentence explaining what remains theoretically misread, practically misjudged, or causally unresolved if the omission persists.
- If using a dual-gap structure, make the first gap primary and the second gap an escalator, not a detour.
- Prefer framing the deficiency as a puzzle, paradox, controversy, or unresolved relationship, not a minor gap.
- In generic social-science mode, use lighter labels such as `mixed`, `underspecified`, `scope-limited`, or `not yet explained` when they fit the field better than stronger problematization language.
- Do not open the paragraph with a citation dump. Open with the paragraph's point.
- Make sure the support answers the first key reader question raised by the paragraph's claim, not a different side issue.
- Do not stop after literature support. Add an interpretive sentence and a bridge into the paper move.
- Use a research-question hinge only when it sharpens the transition; do not tack on a generic question after an already clear gap.
- Avoid writing literature support sentences that merely announce authors or publication history without summarizing the substantive insight.

## Inputs

- current literature notes
- the user's intended new angle
- any known disagreement or contradiction in the literature

## Workflow

1. Decide whether the conversation is best established through synthesized coherence, progressive coherence, or noncoherence.
2. Synthesize the literature into one or two claims about what is already established.
3. In management-journal mode, state where the conversation becomes underspecified, internally contradictory, or theoretically incomplete.
4. In theory-tension mode, emphasize the theoretical inconsistency or mechanism ambiguity.
5. In empirical-contribution mode, emphasize the unresolved relationship and why prior evidence has not settled it.
6. In generic social-science mode, state the unresolved issue in the field's natural language; use formal problematization labels only if they clarify the paragraph.
7. Choose the problematization level: incompleteness, inadequacy, or incommensurability when those labels help.
8. Decide whether the argument needs a second, reinforcing gap such as change over time, decay, or a boundary condition.
9. Name the exact point where the literature stops being persuasive or complete.
10. Decide whether the handoff is strongest as a declarative blind spot, a direct consequence sentence, or as a direct research-question hinge.
11. If helpful, formulate the focal puzzle as one direct paradoxical question or two linked questions when the first question naturally creates a consequence question.
12. Draft one paragraph that makes the blind spot unavoidable. Use one gap or dual-gap stacking depending on what best sharpens the paper's move.
13. Add one sentence that converts the blind spot into stakes before the paper move if the stakes are not already obvious.
14. Make sure the closing sentence opens directly into the paper-move or contribution paragraph.
15. If the paragraph uses multiple literature support sentences, sequence them from general conversation to closest anchor to unresolved issue.

## Output

Return one paragraph that:

- starts from the thematic state of knowledge
- pivots with a sharp transition
- ends on the specific gap that the paper will address
- can end on a direct research question when that creates a stronger hinge into stage 3
- optionally layers a second, reinforcing gap when that makes the contribution more consequential
- makes clear, at least implicitly, how the conversation is being established and problematized
