# Empirical-Contribution Mode

Load this overlay for `AMJ`, `SMJ`, and `Journal of Operations Management`.

## Core Rule

Open on a consequential phenomenon or unresolved relationship, then make the paper's answer and empirical basis visible early. The introduction should show both why the question matters and why the evidence is worth believing.

AMJ-style empirical introductions often work by setting up a paradoxical question: prior theory or common expectations point one way, but visible cases, inconsistent evidence, or puzzling outcomes suggest the relationship is not that simple.

## What To Prioritize

- A bounded, high-value research question
- A clear statement of what the paper shows
- A visible link between the gap and the empirical design
- Contribution framing that is concrete rather than inflated

## Front-End Shape

### Paragraph 1

- Introduce a consequential phenomenon, decision problem, or tension.
- Show why the issue matters for management, strategy, or operations research.
- Prefer a phenomenon-plus-question opening over vague importance claims.
- If useful, juxtapose dominant expectations with a real-world pattern that makes those expectations doubtful.

### Paragraph 2

- Position the exact conversation in the literature.
- State what is known and what remains unresolved in a way that points directly to the paper's question.
- Keep the gap narrow enough that the reader can see what evidence would resolve it.
- Prefer a concrete puzzle or unresolved relationship over a generic claim that the literature is `limited`.
- Consider crystallizing the tension in one paradoxical question sentence.

### Paragraph 3

- State what the paper examines or shows.
- Name the data, design, identification logic, or analytical approach early enough to justify the claim.
- Show why the finding or answer should shift the conversation.
- Make clear what readers will learn, not just what the paper does.
- In AMJ-style drafts, a visible contribution paragraph can follow or be folded into this paragraph if it helps clarify what the paper adds to each literature.

### Optional Paragraph 4

- Briefly preview the sample, setting, time period, or analytical design when that strengthens credibility.
- Use this paragraph to orient the reader before the theory section, not to dump methods.

## Journal Tilt

### AMJ

- Balance broad management relevance with bounded contribution.
- Make the paper's central move visible without overselling.
- Show why the result matters beyond one empirical niche.
- Visible contribution statements are often acceptable and even helpful when they cleanly differentiate the paper's additions.
- A short study preview before the theory section can work well if it sharpens credibility rather than bloating the front end.

### SMJ

- Tie the introduction to a strategic conversation such as capabilities, competition, governance, positioning, adaptation, or value capture.
- Make the mechanism relevant to strategic choice or strategic consequences.
- Keep the contribution anchored in strategy theory, not just in an interesting market setting.

### Journal of Operations Management

- Connect the introduction to operations phenomena such as coordination, execution, resilience, quality, process design, or improvement.
- Show why the paper advances operations theory rather than only describing an operational problem.
- Keep construct clarity and empirical credibility tightly aligned.

## Failure Modes

- The introduction sounds important but never states the paper's answer
- The design appears too late to support the claim
- The contribution is framed as only a new setting, context, or dataset
- The paper promises theory implications that the evidence cannot carry
- The paradox is implied but never clearly articulated
- The contribution paragraph becomes a shopping list instead of a bounded argument
