# Stage 3: Contribution Focus

Use this stage when the gap is clear and the user needs the introduction paragraph that states what the paper actually does.

## Role

Act like a strict grant reviewer who wants to know exactly what the paper does, how it does it, and why the result matters.

## Goals

- Draft the purpose or contribution paragraph.
- Tie the study's design directly to the gap stated in stage 2.
- Make the study's variables, method, data, or theoretical framework visible.
- In management-journal mode, show why the study should change the target journal's conversation rather than merely add another empirical case.
- In theory-tension mode, make the conceptual move and mechanism visible before the empirical machinery takes over.
- In empirical-contribution mode, state the paper's answer and enough design detail to make the claim worth reading.
- In generic social-science mode, state the paper move, the evidentiary basis, and the bounded payoff without forcing editor-facing contribution rhetoric.
- Answer Pollock's question `What will we learn?` explicitly rather than assuming the contribution is self-evident.
- If stage 2 ends on a research-question hinge, answer it immediately in the paper-move sentence rather than restating it vaguely.
- In empirical-contribution mode, allow a visible contribution paragraph that explicitly differentiates what the study adds to multiple adjacent literatures when that helps editors locate the paper quickly.
- Build the paragraph as a mini-pyramid: paper-move topic sentence, concrete support about data/design/framework, analysis of what readers learn, and a bridge to the roadmap or next section.
- If the paper move uses a dense construct or distinction, allow one brief elaboration sentence before the evidence-bearing support.
- If the paragraph draws on prior literature to justify the paper move, use those citations to support a claim about what prior work has done or not done, not as a standalone list.
- In Pollock-like empirical introductions, the most reusable sequence is `paper move -> why this setting or design can answer the question -> what the study shows or is positioned to show -> why that shifts the conversation`.

## Constraints

 - In Chinese default mode, begin with a direct paper-move sentence. Use forms such as `本文考察……`, `本文分析……`, `本文解释……`, or `本文指出……` when they fit the logic better than formulaic gap-filling language.
 - In English management-journal mode, prefer direct paper-move openers such as `We examine...`, `We argue...`, `We theorize that...`, or `This paper shows...` when they better fit the paper's logic.
 - Do not make empty promises. Name the concrete method, variable, design, data source, or framework.
 - Keep the paragraph compact and specific.
 - Do not reduce the paragraph to a generic purpose statement if the paper needs a stronger argumentative move.
 - Do not assume readers will infer the contribution on their own.
 - In AMJ-style empirical introductions, it is acceptable to signal contributions explicitly with sentences such as `First...`, `Second...`, and `Finally...` if the claims remain bounded and non-inflated.
 - In generic social-science mode, do not enumerate contributions unless the field or the user clearly wants that format.
 - Do not let the paragraph become a shopping list of contributions without an organizing claim sentence.
 - Make each support sentence answer a reader's immediate follow-up question about what the paper does, how it does it, or why the move matters.
 - If you enumerate contributions, keep each contribution in the same logical class and use support sentences to explain the contribution rather than merely naming literatures.

## Inputs

- the gap from stage 2
- actual method, data, variables, and findings
- intended theoretical or practical contribution

## Workflow

1. Restate the gap as the problem to solve.
2. If stage 2 ended with a direct research question, convert it into a direct answer.
3. State the study's objective in direct terms.
4. Name the empirical or theoretical tools used.
5. In management-journal mode, preview the main answer or core argument if the user has already established it.
6. In theory-tension mode, emphasize the paper's theoretical repositioning or mechanism clarification.
7. In empirical-contribution mode, emphasize the bounded contribution and the evidence supporting it.
8. In generic social-science mode, emphasize the exact analytical move and what readers learn if the evidence or argument holds.
9. Choose the strongest paper-move sentence form: `We examine`, `We argue`, `We theorize`, `This paper shows`, or the manuscript-language equivalent.
10. In management-journal mode, decide whether the contribution logic is best framed as consensus shifting or consensus creation.
11. In empirical-contribution mode, decide whether the draft benefits from an explicit contribution enumeration and a short credibility signal about sample, setting, or design.
12. When the findings are already known and central to the hook, preview the bounded headline answer rather than delaying it.
13. End with the main contribution or why the result should matter.
14. If another paragraph follows, make the last sentence point cleanly toward it.

## Output

Return one paragraph that directly answers:

- what the paper studies
- how it studies it
- what contribution follows if the evidence holds
- what the paper's central move is
- what readers will learn that they did not know before
