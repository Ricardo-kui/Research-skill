# Management-Journal Mode

Load this overlay when the target outlet is `ASQ`, `AMJ`, `SMJ`, `Organization Science`, `Journal of Operations Management`, or a similar English management journal.

Then route to one of two submodes:

- `ASQ` / `Organization Science` -> load `management-theory-tension-mode.md`
- `AMJ` / `SMJ` / `Journal of Operations Management` -> load `management-empirical-contribution-mode.md`

## Core Rule

Fit and contribution come before polish. The introduction must show not just that the topic is interesting, but that the paper belongs in a specific conversation and changes what that conversation should believe.

## Non-Negotiables

- Name the conversation, not just the topic.
- Make the introduction answer `Who cares?`, `What do we know / not know / so what?`, and `What will we learn?`
- Make the theoretical tension legible early.
- Show why the result would move a reader's prior belief.
- Do not let sample novelty, context novelty, or method novelty stand in for contribution.
- Keep claim strength aligned with the evidence.
- Prefer puzzles, paradoxes, controversies, or unresolved relationships over generic gap-filling rhetoric.

## Front-End Test

By the end of the first three paragraphs, a management-journal reader should be able to answer:

1. What phenomenon, puzzle, or tension is this paper about?
2. What does the literature already claim?
3. What exactly is missing, mis-specified, or unresolved?
4. What does this paper show or argue?
5. Why should this change the conversation?

If those answers are still fuzzy, the introduction is not ready.

## Writing Rules

- Default to English prose for manuscript-facing output, even if the user discusses the project in Chinese.
- Prefer "This paper examines...", "We argue that...", "We show that..." over vague future-tense promises.
- State the paper's move before the roadmap.
- Use literature to position the conversation, not to warehouse citations.
- Make boundary conditions and mechanism visible when they are central to the contribution.

## Failure Modes

- The introduction sounds like a report on an important topic rather than a paper intervening in a debate.
- The gap is generic and could fit many unrelated papers.
- The contribution is described only as a new dataset, setting, method, or context.
- The prose promises more than the design can support.
- The roadmap arrives before the paper's move is clear.
