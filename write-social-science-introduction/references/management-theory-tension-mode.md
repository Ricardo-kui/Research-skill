# Theory-Tension Mode

Load this overlay for `ASQ` and `Organization Science`.

## Core Rule

Open on a theoretically consequential puzzle, contradiction, or organizing tension. The introduction should persuade the reader that the paper changes how the phenomenon should be understood, not merely that the setting is important.

Do not collapse the front end into a bare `X-Y-M` schema before the reader can see the conversation, the tension, and the paper's conceptual move.

## What To Prioritize

- Conceptual novelty over sample novelty
- Mechanism and assumptions over method-first exposition
- A sharp theoretical question over broad managerial relevance claims
- An argument that shifts how the conversation is framed
- Theoretical sequencing over regression-style variable labeling

## Front-End Shape

### Paragraph 1

- Surface a puzzle, contradiction, or process tension.
- Show why the phenomenon exposes a limitation in prevailing theory.
- Avoid sounding like a general-interest business trend article.

### Paragraph 2

- Name the conversation precisely.
- State what current theory gets partly right and where it becomes too coarse, one-sided, or internally inconsistent.
- Turn the literature into a conceptual problem, not a citation tour.

### Paragraph 3

- State the paper's central argument or theoretical move.
- If there is empirical evidence, use it to support the conceptual move rather than to dominate the paragraph.
- Keep the mechanism visible.
- Prefer a paper-move sentence such as `We argue...`, `We theorize that...`, or `This paper shows...` when it clarifies the theoretical intervention.

## Journal Tilt

### ASQ

- Favor assumption-challenging insights, process explanations, and conceptual repositioning.
- Make clear why the paper matters beyond one narrow empirical context.
- Let the introduction sound like an intervention in organization theory.

### Organization Science

- Favor conceptually precise, mechanism-rich framing.
- Keep interdisciplinary relevance under control; it should sharpen the contribution, not blur it.
- Make the organizing logic highly visible.

## Failure Modes

- Leading with an important context but no real theoretical problem
- Treating a new dataset or field site as the main contribution
- Postponing the paper's conceptual move until after design detail
- Using abstract claims that do not specify which assumption or mechanism is under pressure
- Turning the introduction into an `X causes Y under M` skeleton before the theory has been made legible
