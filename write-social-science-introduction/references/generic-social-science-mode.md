# Generic Social-Science Mode

Load this overlay when the user wants a social-science introduction but the target outlet is not clearly a management, strategy, organization, or operations journal.

## Core Rule

Keep the funnel, but do not force management-journal rhetoric. The introduction should make the question, current knowledge, blind spot, and paper move legible in the target field's own register.

## What To Prioritize

- A clear research question or substantive puzzle
- A precise statement of what prior work already establishes
- A bounded blind spot rather than a ceremonial `gap`
- A visible paper move: argument, evidence, scope, or mechanism
- Claim strength that matches the available evidence

## Writing Rules

- Use the manuscript language unless the user requests another language.
- Name the problem directly instead of imitating a target journal's house style.
- Prefer field-appropriate importance claims over imported editor-facing rhetoric.
- State what the paper does and what readers learn without pretending every paper must `shift a conversation`.
- Use literature to establish the state of knowledge, not to perform sophistication.
- If the field is theory-light or evidence-first, let the prose reflect that instead of forcing a theory-tension frame.

## Hook Guidance

- Prefer one of three openings:
  - a consequential empirical pattern
  - a substantive unresolved question
  - a field-level consensus that now exposes a narrow unresolved issue
- Do not force paradox language if the field usually frames the problem as ambiguity, scope limitation, mechanism uncertainty, or inconsistent findings.

## Literature-Turn Guidance

- State what the literature collectively shows.
- Then identify exactly where it stops being persuasive, complete, or well specified.
- Use labels such as `incomplete`, `underspecified`, `mixed`, `inconsistent`, or `scope-limited` when they fit better than stronger problematization language.
- Use conversation / problematization terminology only when it sharpens the logic rather than sounding imported.

## Contribution Guidance

- Make the paper move concrete:
  - what the paper examines, argues, estimates, explains, or compares
  - what evidence, data, design, or theoretical material it uses
  - what readers learn if the paper is correct
- Avoid contribution shopping lists unless the target field strongly expects them.
- Prefer one organizing claim over three weak mini-claims.

## QC Test

By the end of the first three substantive paragraphs, a reader in the target field should be able to answer:

1. What question or problem is this paper addressing?
2. What does prior work already tell us?
3. What remains unresolved in exact terms?
4. What does this paper do about it?
5. Why is that move worth reading?

If those answers are still fuzzy, the introduction is not ready.

## Failure Modes

- The prose imitates management-journal rhetoric without outlet fit
- The introduction overuses `gap` language without naming the blind spot
- The contribution is generic because the paper move is not concrete
- The literature paragraph becomes a mini literature review instead of a turn toward the paper
- The introduction sounds polished but never states what the paper actually does
