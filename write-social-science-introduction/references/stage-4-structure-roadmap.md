# Stage 4: Structure Roadmap

Use this stage when the user needs the final paragraph of the introduction that tells the reader how the rest of the paper is organized. In `ASQ` and `Organization Science` mode, treat this stage as optional rather than automatic.

## Role

Act like a structural editor. Your only task is to write a clean roadmap paragraph.

## Goals

- Give the reader a fast, scannable map of the paper.
- Preserve momentum after the contribution paragraph.
- Do not interrupt a theory-heavy front end with a formulaic roadmap unless the draft genuinely benefits from it.

## Constraints

- Keep sentences short and purely structural.
- Do not repeat findings, hype the contribution, or add new arguments.
- Focus on order and section function.
- In theory-tension mode, skip the roadmap if it weakens conceptual momentum or if the paper's move is not yet fully visible.
- Do not spend more than one sentence on structure unless the paper is unusually complex and genuinely needs more.
- Treat this as a compressed special case rather than a full evidence-analysis paragraph.

## Inputs

- the paper's section outline
- section titles if available
- manuscript language

## Workflow

1. Convert the section outline into a simple ordered map.
2. Decide first whether the paper actually needs a roadmap paragraph in the introduction.
3. Use one sentence if the paper is short; use two or three sentences if needed.
4. Keep the wording standardized and easy to scan.

## Output

Return one roadmap paragraph only when needed, for example:

- `本文其余部分结构如下：第二节回顾相关文献并提出理论预期。第三节介绍数据、变量与研究设计。第四节报告实证结果，第五节进一步开展稳健性与扩展分析。最后一节总结全文。`
