# Contribution Paragraph Shell

Use this template when drafting Stage 3 (contribution focus). The goal is one tight paragraph that answers Pollock's third question: "What will we learn?"

---

## Outlet-specific defaults

| Outlet | Primary emphasis | Optional elements |
|---|---|---|
| `AMJ / SMJ / JOM` | Paper move + design/data signal + bounded contribution | Explicit "First…Second…" enumeration if multiple adjacent literatures are addressed |
| `ASQ / Organization Science` | Conceptual move + mechanism or assumption revision | Avoid over-enumeration; let the argument carry the contribution |
| `Generic social-science` | Paper move + analytical approach + bounded payoff | No forced management-journal rhetoric |

---

## Shell

```
[PAPER-MOVE SENTENCE — state directly what the paper does.
 Choose the opener that fits the paper's logic:
 "We examine…", "We argue…", "We theorize that…", "We show…",
 "This paper investigates…", "This study asks…"
 Do NOT use "This paper aims to fill the gap identified above." That is circular filler.]

[DESIGN OR FRAMEWORK SIGNAL — one sentence naming the concrete method, data, setting,
 or theoretical framework. This gives the paper-move sentence credibility.
 Do NOT leave it vague: "using a large dataset" is not enough.
 Pattern: "Drawing on [data / theory / N observations from X setting], we..."]

[HEADLINE ANSWER or ARGUMENT — one sentence previewing what the paper shows or argues,
 in bounded terms.
 Pattern: "We find that [result], contingent on [condition]." OR
 "We argue that [mechanism] explains [phenomenon] when [boundary condition]."]

[CONTRIBUTION STATEMENT — one or two sentences explaining what the paper changes
 in the conversation. Choose one logic:
 - "consensus shifting": "These findings challenge the assumption that [X]..."
 - "consensus creation": "By integrating [stream A] and [stream B], we clarify..."
 - "scope extension": "We show that [known mechanism] operates differently in [new context]..."]

[BRIDGE (optional) — one sentence pointing to the roadmap or the next major section.
 Only needed if the contribution paragraph comes before a long paper.]
```

---

## AMJ-style enumeration variant (when contribution is multi-part)

Use only when the paper speaks to two or more clearly distinct adjacent literatures and enumeration genuinely helps editors locate the paper:

```
[PAPER-MOVE SENTENCE]
[DESIGN SIGNAL]
[HEADLINE ANSWER]

"This study makes three contributions. First, [contribution to stream A].
Second, [contribution to stream B]. Finally, [methodological or contextual contribution]."
```

**Caution**: keep each contribution in the same logical class. Do not mix "we find X" with "we introduce Y" with "we replicate in context Z" as co-equal contributions unless they are genuinely parallel.

---

## Patterns to avoid

| Anti-pattern | Example | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Circular paper-move | "This study fills the gap above by studying X." | Name what the study actually *does*, not the gap it fills |
| Empty design signal | "Using a large longitudinal dataset..." | Name the source, N, and setting: "Using a panel of 3,200 firm-year observations from..." |
| Overclaiming | "This fundamentally transforms how we understand X." | Bound the claim: "...in contexts where [condition] holds" |
| Orphan contribution | Final paragraph claims a contribution not prepared anywhere earlier | Trace back: every contribution in Stage 3 must have a setup in Stage 2 |
| Contribution list without organizer | "First… Second… Third… Fourth…" (four or more) | Cap at three; combine minor contributions or move them to Discussion |

---

## Pollock four-question self-check

After drafting, verify the paragraph answers all four:

1. [ ] What does the paper examine?
2. [ ] Why can this setting or design answer the question?
3. [ ] What does the paper show or argue?
4. [ ] What should change in the conversation because of that answer?

If any answer is missing, the paragraph is not yet a contribution paragraph — it is a purpose statement.
