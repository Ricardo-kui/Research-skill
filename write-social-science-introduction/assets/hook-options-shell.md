# Hook Options Shell

Use this template when drafting Stage 1 hook options. Always produce at least two options (A + B); add Option C when an academic-conversation hook is strong or when the outlet is ASQ/OS.

Identify outlet first:
- `AMJ / SMJ / JOM` → bias toward Option A (real-world trend or consequential phenomenon)
- `ASQ / Organization Science` → bias toward Option B or C (paradox, contradiction, or mature academic conversation)

---

## Option A — 事实 / 数据驱动型 (Real-World Trend or Fact)

```
Hook subtype: real-world trend / anecdote / policy shift

[OPENING FACT OR SCENE — one sentence anchoring the phenomenon in a concrete, verifiable
 observation. Do not invent data. If no fact is available, use Option B instead.
 Pattern: "By [year], [X] had [verifiable trend or scale]."]

[OPENING PARAPHRASE — convert the fact into the paper's underlying tension or mechanism.
 Do not linger in scene-setting. One sentence.
 Pattern: "Yet [surprising or counterintuitive implication] remains unexplained." OR
 "[Fact] raises a question that prior research has not resolved: [question]."]

[FOCAL QUESTION — one sentence stating what the paper is really about.
 Optional if the paraphrase already implies it clearly.]

[BRIDGE — one sentence pointing toward the literature turn.]
```

**Length target**: 70–110 words (English) / ~150 characters (Chinese).

---

## Option B — 理论悖论型 (Paradox or Contradiction)

```
Hook subtype: academic trend / rhetorical question / non-coherence

[TENSION STATEMENT — one sentence stating the theoretical contradiction or empirical puzzle.
 Pattern: "Despite [well-established expectation], evidence consistently shows [opposite]."]

[OPENING PARAPHRASE — explain why the tension is not a trivial measurement issue
 but a genuine theoretical problem.
 Pattern: "This paradox suggests that [standard mechanism] may be [insufficient / incomplete /
 context-dependent]."]

[FOCAL QUESTION — make the research question unavoidable.
 Pattern: "When and why does [mechanism] break down?"]

[BRIDGE — one sentence pointing toward the literature turn.]
```

---

## Option C — 学术对话 / 共识型 (Academic-Conversation Hook)

```
Hook subtype: academic trend / synthesized coherence / progressive coherence

[CONVERSATION STATEMENT — name the mature scholarly conversation.
 Pattern: "Scholars in [field] have devoted considerable attention to [X], establishing
 that [core consensus]."]

[OPENING PARAPHRASE — convert the conversation into a sharp insufficiency.
 Pattern: "Yet despite this progress, a consequential question remains: [question]." OR
 "This body of work has not examined [specific blind spot], leaving [practical/theoretical
 implication] unresolved."]

[FOCAL QUESTION — state the unresolved issue in one sentence.]

[BRIDGE — one sentence pointing toward the literature turn.]
```

---

## Anti-patterns to avoid in Stage 1

| Anti-pattern | Example | Why problematic |
|---|---|---|
| Time-marker filler | "近年来，随着X的发展…" | Signals no concrete anchor; most management editors reject on sight |
| Atmosphere without argument | Three vivid sentences that don't generate a question | Hook consumed space but never converted into tension |
| Invented data | "Studies show 73% of firms…" without a citation | Fabricated facts destroy credibility with reviewers |
| Rhetorical question stack | "What makes teams effective? Why do some fail? Is leadership the key?" | Multiple weak questions dilute focus; one strong question beats three weak ones |
| Quote as decoration | Opening quote that is never echoed later | Pollock: use quotes only if they can be referenced again in findings/implications |

---

## Checklist before submitting Stage 1 options

- [ ] At least one option anchors in a concrete, verifiable fact or well-established consensus
- [ ] Every option converts the hook into an argument or tension within 2 sentences
- [ ] No option uses generic time-marker language
- [ ] The focal research question is visible or strongly implied
- [ ] The paragraph ends pointing toward the literature turn, not dangling in atmosphere
