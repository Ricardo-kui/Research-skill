# Gap Paragraph Shell

Use this template when drafting Stage 2 (literature turn). The goal is a single paragraph that moves from "what we know" to "exactly what we do not know and why that matters."

---

## Decision: Choose the establishing mode first

| Mode | Use when | Typical signal phrases |
|---|---|---|
| **Synthesized coherence** | Two separate streams share a latent theme that has not been linked | "both…and…", "underlying each of these", "common to these accounts" |
| **Progressive coherence** | One stream has evolved and now needs to move forward | "early work… more recently…", "a growing body of research" |
| **Non-coherence** | Conflicting findings or incompatible views need adjudication | "on the one hand…on the other", "conflicting evidence", "scholars disagree" |

## Decision: Choose the problematization level

| Level | Claim | Use when |
|---|---|---|
| **Incompleteness** | More needs to be said; the omission matters | The paper adds a missing piece while respecting prior work |
| **Inadequacy** | The current framing is partial or insufficient | The paper reframes the phenomenon, not just adds to it |
| **Incommensurability** | The dominant view is seriously flawed | The paper challenges a core assumption; high-reward, high-risk |

Default: **incompleteness** or **inadequacy** unless the paper genuinely requires confrontational reframing.

---

## Shell

```
[THEMATIC CLAIM — open with what the literature has established, in one thematic sentence.
 Do NOT open with citations. Open with the point.
 Pattern: "Research on [X] has established that [core finding or consensus]."]

[OPTIONAL NARROWING — one sentence clarifying the scope of the conversation,
 especially when two streams are being bridged or the literature is large.]

[SYNTHESIZED SUPPORT — 1–2 sentences citing evidence that supports the opening claim.
 Do not list papers; state what they collectively show.
 Pattern: "Studies across [domain] consistently find [result] (Author, Year; Author, Year)."]

[GAP PIVOT — one sharp sentence naming the exact deficiency.
 Use precise verbs: ignores / conflates / cannot explain / remains silent on /
 assumes away / leaves unresolved.
 Do NOT use vague language like "existing research is limited."]

[GAP VALUE SENTENCE — one sentence explaining what remains theoretically unstable,
 practically misjudged, or causally ambiguous because of the omission.
 Pattern: "Without understanding [gap], [theoretical inference / practical evaluation /
 causal interpretation] remains at risk of [error / distortion / overgeneralization]."]

[HINGE (optional) — a direct research question that crystallizes the puzzle and
 opens naturally into Stage 3.
 Use only when it sharpens the transition; do not tack on if the gap is already clear.
 Pattern: "We therefore ask: [direct question]?"]
```

---

## Dual-gap structure (when needed)

Use when the paper makes a two-step argument: the first gap creates the need, the second gap intensifies it.

```
[PRIMARY GAP — as above]
[ESCALATING GAP — one sentence that adds urgency or boundary to the primary gap.
 Pattern: "This gap is compounded by [temporal change / boundary condition / asymmetry]
 that prior work has not addressed."]
[GAP VALUE SENTENCE — covers both gaps]
```

Keep the second gap as an escalator, not a detour.

---

## Sentence-level patterns for the gap pivot

```
"Despite this progress, [phenomenon / relationship / mechanism] remains [unexplained / untested / contested]."
"Existing accounts cannot explain [specific empirical pattern or contradiction]."
"Prior work has focused on [X] while overlooking [Y], leaving [implication] unclear."
"The literature assumes [assumption], yet this assumption may not hold when [condition]."
"We know [A], but we do not yet understand [B], which matters because [C]."
```

---

## Checklist before submitting Stage 2

- [ ] Opens with a thematic claim, not a citation list
- [ ] Establishing mode is chosen (synthesized / progressive / non-coherence)
- [ ] Problematization level is chosen (incompleteness / inadequacy / incommensurability)
- [ ] Gap pivot uses a precise verb, not vague "limited" language
- [ ] Gap-value sentence makes the theoretical or practical stakes explicit
- [ ] Paragraph ends on a clean handoff into Stage 3 (contribution paragraph)
- [ ] No author-by-author listing unless a literature map was explicitly requested
