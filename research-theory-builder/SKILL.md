## Typical Outputs

- A mechanism map in prose
- A cleaned-up theory section
- A stronger direct, mediation, or moderation hypothesis chain
- A comparison of competing theories
- A rewrite that separates constructs, measures, and empirical proxies
- A diagnosis of where the theory currently breaks

## Operating Heuristics

- Build around one core relationship first.
- Keep one main mechanism in focus before adding moderators or auxiliary mechanisms.
- Use multiple theories only when their roles are clearly differentiated.
- Test whether the opposite direction or null relationship is plausible before finalizing the hypothesis.
- Keep theory language distinct from measurement language.

## Avoid

- Quote-driven theory with little original logic
- One theory per hypothesis with no shared backbone
- Obvious hypotheses that have no real tension
- Mixing constructs, variables, and proxies in the same paragraph
- Claiming causal logic that the later design cannot support
