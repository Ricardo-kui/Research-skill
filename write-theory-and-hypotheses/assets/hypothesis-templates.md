# Hypothesis Templates

Reference these sentence patterns when drafting or rewriting hypotheses. Match the relationship type first, then fill in the constructs.

---

## Type 1: Direct / Continuous Positive

```
Hypothesis X: [IV] is positively related to [DV].
```

When DV is time-to-event or delay (conceptually "faster is better"):
```
Hypothesis X: [IV] is positively related to the speed of [DV outcome],
such that higher [IV] is associated with shorter time to [event].
```
> Operational note: if modeling duration or hazard, confirm whether a positive coefficient means faster or slower in your estimator before writing the direction.

---

## Type 2: Direct Negative

```
Hypothesis X: [IV] is negatively related to [DV].
```

---

## Type 3: Moderation — Strengthening

```
Hypothesis X: [Moderator] moderates the relationship between [IV] and [DV],
such that the positive (negative) relationship is stronger when [Moderator] is high
than when [Moderator] is low.
```

---

## Type 4: Moderation — Weakening / Buffering

```
Hypothesis X: [Moderator] weakens the positive (negative) relationship between [IV] and [DV],
such that the relationship is attenuated when [Moderator] is high.
```

---

## Type 5: Mediation

```
Hypothesis X: [Mediator] mediates the relationship between [IV] and [DV],
such that [IV] affects [DV] indirectly through its effect on [Mediator].
```

If partial mediation is expected:
```
Hypothesis X: [Mediator] partially mediates the relationship between [IV] and [DV].
```

---

## Type 6: Curvilinear / Inverted-U

```
Hypothesis X: The relationship between [IV] and [DV] follows an inverted-U shape,
such that [DV] increases with [IV] up to a threshold and then decreases.
```

---

## Type 7: Comparison / Group Difference

```
Hypothesis X: [Group A] exhibits higher (lower) [DV] than [Group B].
```

---

## Type 8: Conditional Main Effect (Three-Way Setup)

```
Hypothesis X: The moderating effect of [Moderator 1] on the [IV]–[DV] relationship
is itself moderated by [Moderator 2], such that the interaction is stronger (weaker)
when [Moderator 2] is high.
```

---

## Common Wording Checklist

- [ ] Direction stated explicitly (positive / negative / stronger / weaker)
- [ ] Construct names in hypothesis match construct names in theory section
- [ ] If DV is time-based, operational sign is consistent with conceptual direction
- [ ] Moderation states the *altered strength or direction*, not just "moderates"
- [ ] Mediation states the *pathway*, not just "mediates"
