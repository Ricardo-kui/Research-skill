# Mechanism Paragraph Shell

Use this scaffold when drafting a mechanism paragraph from scratch. Fill in the bracketed slots; do not leave any slot empty before submitting.

---

## Shell

```
[CLAIM — state the focal relationship in one sentence, present tense, active voice.]

[NARROWING — optional: scope or condition under which the claim holds, if needed.]

[SUPPORT — cite 1–2 pieces of evidence or theory that back the mechanism.
 Do NOT use citations as a substitute for explanation. Each citation earns its place by
 supporting a specific claim about why the mechanism works.]

[ANALYSIS — explain the causal logic: what is the actor, what is the resource or
 constraint, what is the process that connects IV to DV? This is the "why" paragraph.
 Avoid: "prior research shows X." Prefer: "because [actor] faces [condition],
 [action/process] follows, which leads to [outcome]."]

[HYPOTHESIS BRIDGE — end with one sentence that makes the empirical prediction
 explicit. Use: "Therefore, we predict that..." or "This logic leads to Hypothesis X."]
```

---

## Filled Example (placeholder constructs)

> [CLAIM] Firms with higher knowledge diversity generate more novel solutions to technical problems.
>
> [SUPPORT] Knowledge diversity broadens the set of analogies and recombinations available to problem solvers (Hargadon & Sutton, 1997; Fleming, 2001).
>
> [ANALYSIS] When individuals with different knowledge bases collaborate, each brings domain-specific schemas that the other lacks. This variety increases the probability that at least one team member recognizes a non-obvious solution path. The mechanism is search breadth: diverse teams explore a wider solution space per unit of effort than homogeneous teams.
>
> [BRIDGE] We therefore predict that knowledge diversity is positively related to the novelty of firm-level inventions (Hypothesis 1).

---

## Anti-patterns to avoid

| Anti-pattern | What it looks like | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Citation dump | "X, Y, and Z (2001, 2005, 2010) all show that A leads to B." | Explain *why* A leads to B; use one citation to support the mechanism |
| Tautology | "Because firms with more resources have more resources to invest..." | Identify the actual mechanism (slack → risk tolerance → exploration) |
| Missing actor | "When competition increases, performance decreases." | Who does what? Managers reduce R&D? Customers defect? |
| Overcrowded paragraph | Two mechanisms in one paragraph | One paragraph = one mechanism chain |
